# Forex Trading Knowledge Base

## What is Forex?

Forex (Foreign Exchange Market) is the global marketplace where currencies are bought and sold. It is the largest financial market in the world, with daily trading volumes exceeding trillions of dollars.

## Major Currency Pairs

### EUR/USD

Euro against US Dollar. Most traded currency pair worldwide.

### GBP/USD

British Pound against US Dollar. Known for high volatility.

### USD/JPY

US Dollar against Japanese Yen. Influenced by Bank of Japan policies.

### USD/CHF

US Dollar against Swiss Franc. Considered a safe-haven currency pair.

### AUD/USD

Australian Dollar against US Dollar. Influenced by commodity prices.

### USD/CAD

US Dollar against Canadian Dollar. Influenced by oil prices.

## Basic Forex Terms

### Pip

Smallest price movement in a currency pair.

### Lot

Standard trading unit in forex.

### Spread

Difference between bid and ask price.

### Leverage

Borrowed capital used to increase trading exposure.

### Margin

Amount required to open a leveraged position.

## Types of Orders

### Market Order

Executed immediately at current market price.

### Limit Order

Executed at a specified price or better.

### Stop Order

Executed when price reaches a specified level.

### Stop Loss

Automatically closes a trade to limit losses.

### Take Profit

Automatically closes a trade to lock profits.

## Fundamental Factors Affecting Forex

### Interest Rates

Higher interest rates often strengthen a currency.

### Inflation

High inflation generally weakens a currency.

### GDP Growth

Strong economic growth usually supports currency appreciation.

### Employment Data

Strong employment numbers can strengthen a currency.

### Central Bank Decisions

Federal Reserve, ECB, BOJ, and BOE decisions significantly impact forex markets.

## Risk Management

### Risk Per Trade

Professional traders usually risk 1-2% of capital per trade.

### Risk-Reward Ratio

A common target is 1:2 or higher.

### Diversification

Avoid concentrating all trades in one currency pair.

### Emotional Control

Successful trading requires discipline and adherence to a trading plan.

## Common Trading Strategies

### Trend Following

Trading in the direction of the prevailing market trend.

### Breakout Trading

Entering trades when price breaks key support or resistance levels.

### Swing Trading

Holding trades for several days to capture medium-term movements.

### Scalping

Executing multiple short-term trades for small profits.

## Technical Indicators

### RSI

Measures momentum and identifies overbought or oversold conditions.

### MACD

Trend-following momentum indicator.

### Moving Average

Shows average price over a specific period.

### Bollinger Bands

Measures market volatility.

### ATR

Measures average market volatility.

### Shooting Star

### Doji

### Bearish Engulfing
